Last week, you built your Digital Persona — a short document that introduces you to any AI tool. This week, we're putting it to work.

You're going to have a conversation with Gemini about your actual job. Not hypothetical stuff. Your real tasks, your real frustrations, the things that eat up your time every week. By the end, you'll have a personal roadmap of what AI can help you with — and what it probably shouldn't touch.

Think of it like sitting down with a consultant who wants to understand how you work before giving advice. Except this one is free and available on your phone.

---

## Step 1: Open Gemini and paste your Digital Persona

If you did last week's challenge, you already have a Digital Persona saved somewhere — Notes app, Google Docs, or Google Keep. Open a new chat in Gemini and paste it first. This gives Gemini context about who you are before you even ask anything.

If you haven't done the Digital Persona challenge yet, no worries. You can still do this one. Just skip this step and answer Gemini's questions with a bit more detail about yourself.

---

## Step 2: Paste this prompt right after

> *"Based on what you know about me, I want you to help me discover where AI can save me time at work. Interview me about my daily and weekly tasks — ask me ONE question at a time and wait for my answer before moving on. Ask about: what tasks I do regularly, which ones take the most time, which ones I find repetitive or draining, and what tools I currently use. After the interview, give me a simple 30-60-90 day plan with: quick wins I can start this week, areas where AI can help the most, and things I should keep doing manually."*

If you skipped Step 1, add this before the prompt:

> *"First, a bit about me: I work as {your job title} in {your industry}. My main responsibilities include {two or three things you do regularly}."*

---

## Step 3: Answer like you're explaining your job to a friend over coffee

No need to be formal. Answer the way you'd describe your work to a close friend or relative over dinner. The more real and specific you are, the more useful the plan becomes.

If Gemini asks something too broad, just tell it. Say things like "Can you be more specific?" or "I'm not sure what you mean — can you give me an example?"

---

## Step 4: Push back and ask follow-ups

This is a conversation, not a quiz. If something in the plan doesn't fit your situation, say so. Try things like:

- *"That won't work because we're not allowed to use external tools at work."*
- *"Can you give me a simpler version of that?"*
- *"What about {specific task}? That's the one that really kills my time."*

The more you push, the better the plan gets. Treat it like a real consultation. AI gives better answers when you challenge it.

---

## Step 5: Save your roadmap

Once Gemini gives you the plan, copy it and save it somewhere you'll actually see it — Notes app, Google Docs, Google Keep, wherever works for you.

This is your personal automation roadmap. Check back on it next week and see what you've actually started.

---

## You just learned three real things:

- That AI can look at your workflow and spot opportunities you might not see yourself
- That the best AI advice comes from honest, specific conversations — not generic prompts
- That pushing back on AI makes it work harder for you, not less
