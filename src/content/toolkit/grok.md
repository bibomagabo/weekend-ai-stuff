## Where it helps

- **Breaking news research**: Track how a story is developing in real time before mainstream coverage catches up
- **Trend monitoring**: See what topics are gaining traction in your industry or professional community
- **Sentiment research**: Understand how people are reacting to an announcement, a product launch, or a policy change
- **Current events**: Get context on fast-moving topics where last week's information is already outdated

## What else it can do

- Web search mode for research beyond X content
- Image analysis — upload a screenshot or image and ask questions about it
- Think mode for step-by-step reasoning on more complex questions
- Available directly inside X if you already use the platform regularly

## Tips for better research

- **Use it for what's happening now.** Grok's edge is real-time information. For well-established topics, Perplexity or Gemini will give you more thorough, sourced answers.
- **Compare it to another tool.** Ask the same research question in Grok and Gemini. Where they differ is usually where the story is still developing.
- **Pair it with chained prompting.** The [Research challenge](https://weekend-ai-stuff.vercel.app/challenges/2026-04-04-research-properly/) from this weekend shows you how to go deeper than one question — the same technique works in Grok.

## What makes it different from Perplexity

Both tools search the web and give you direct answers. The difference is the data source. Perplexity pulls from the broader web and cites its sources clearly — it's the better tool for researched, verifiable answers. Grok has a live connection to X, which means it can surface information that hasn't made it to mainstream websites yet. Use Perplexity when you need accuracy and sources. Use Grok when you need to know what's happening right now.
