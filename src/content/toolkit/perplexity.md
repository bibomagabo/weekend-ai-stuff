## Where it helps

- **Background research**: Get a sourced overview of any topic without clicking through multiple websites
- **Fact-checking**: Verify claims before you use them in a report, presentation, or meeting
- **Competitive research**: Ask about trends, tools, or developments in your industry and see where the information comes from
- **Decision support**: Research your options side by side before making a work or personal decision

## What else it can do

- Follow-up questions in the same thread, so your research builds on itself instead of starting over
- Save research threads to come back to later (free account required)
- Upload documents and ask questions about them (paid tier)
- Switch between different AI models depending on the depth of answer you need (paid tier)

## Tips for better research

- **Ask follow-up questions.** Don't stop at the first answer. Ask Perplexity to go deeper on any part that didn't fully click.
- **Check the sources.** The citations are there for a reason. Click through to at least one source before using the information at work.
- **Ask what it's unsure about.** Try adding "What parts of this are still uncertain or debated?" at the end of any research question. You'll get a more honest answer.
- **Pair it with chained prompting.** The [Research challenge](https://weekend-ai-stuff.vercel.app/challenges/2026-04-04-research-properly/) from this weekend walks you through a research technique that works even better when you use Perplexity as your tool.

## What makes it different from Gemini

Gemini is a general-purpose AI assistant — great for writing, brainstorming, and getting things done. Perplexity is purpose-built for research. It always cites its sources, which makes it the right tool when accuracy and traceability matter. Use Gemini when you need help thinking or creating. Use Perplexity when you need answers you can verify.
